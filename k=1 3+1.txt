\begin{abstract}
We present a $(1{+}3)$-dimensional formulation of K\,=\,1 Chronogeometrodynamics
whose central feature is a symplectic gauge collapse from continuous $6$-dimensional
freedom to a discrete three-element pairing class, under the local-inertial-frame
constraint that the cost weight $H_*$ is diagonal. The framework defines an information
time $dt_\mathrm{info} := d\Phi/H$, a Hessian metric $G = \nabla^2(dt_\mathrm{info}^2)|_{K=1}$,
and studies the dynamics of the order parameter $K(x) := x^\top G x$ near the
equilibrium shell $\{K=1\}$.

Three declared inputs are separated from internal deductions throughout:
(i)~Lorentzian signature $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$, established in a companion realizability
paper; (ii)~a canonical symplectic gauge $J_0$, selected by the Route~1 Proposition,
which reduces the $6$-dimensional gauge freedom to a discrete $3$-element set under
the diagonal-$H_*$ constraint; (iii)~an isotropic It\^o SDE for fluctuation analysis.

Within these inputs the internal chain is logically closed. The unique symplectic
generator $J_G = \alpha G^{-1}J_0$ yields a critical damping scalar
$d_c = \alpha/(\kappa\ell)$ for Rindler metrics. In the local radial OU sector
with isotropic noise, $\Var(K)=\sigma^2/(2d_c)$ universally across all Lorentzian $G$.
A Clausius relation $\delta Q = T_\mathrm{eff}\,\delta S$ holds only in the reduced
Class~A radial sector.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{New result (Section~\ref{sec:interface}).}
The minimum symplectic eigenvalue $\sigma_1 = \kappa\ell$ of the Rindler metric
simultaneously determines the critical damping scalar ($d_c = \alpha/\sigma_1$),
the horizon area ($A = 4\pi\sigma_1^2$), and the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy
($S_\mathrm{BH} = \pi\sigma_1^2$). Defining the symplectic area
\[
  Q_\mathrm{boost} := \sigma_1^2 \cdot 4\pi
  = (\kappa\ell)^2 \cdot 4\pi = A_\mathrm{horizon} = 4\,S_\mathrm{BH},
\]
one identifies Class~B tangential diffusion on $\{K=1\}\cong H^3$ not as a gauge
degree of freedom but as the symplectic geometric carrier of Bekenstein--Hawking
entropy. Combined with $\Var(K) = \sigma^2/(2d_c) = T_\mathrm{tol}$, the geometric
closure reads $\Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost} = 2\alpha\sigma_1^2/\ell$.
The J$_0$/J$_4$ equivalence in the Rindler/$2{+}2$ sector is proved algebraically
via the block-product spectrum theorem.
Several identities are numerically verified on five standard Lorentzian metrics.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The $2$-dimensional prototype of K\,=\,1 Chronogeometrodynamics~\cite{proto2d}
established a tight logical chain: from the information-time definition and the
Hessian metric $G$, one derives (i)~a unique symplectic generator $J_G$ (in 2D the
skew space is one-dimensional, so no gauge choice is needed), (ii)~a critical damping
scalar $d_c$ that is positive if and only if $\Sig(G)=(1,1)$, and (iii)~a Clausius
relation in the radial fluctuation sector, supplying one ingredient of the Jacobson
thermodynamic derivation of Einstein's equations~\cite{Jacobson}. In 2D the three conditions $d_c>0$,
$\det G<0$, and $\Sig=(1,1)$ are mutually equivalent; uniqueness of $J_G$ requires
no additional input.

Moving to $(1{+}3)$ dimensions changes the logical structure in two important ways.
First, the skew-symmetric matrix space is six-dimensional, so $G$ alone does not
determine $J_0$ uniquely; a gauge choice is required. Second, the spectral condition
$d_c>0$ is no longer equivalent to $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$: Lorentzian signature implies
$d_c>0$, but $d_c>0$ does not imply $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$, since for example
$\Sig=(3,1)$ also yields $d_c>0$. The present paper makes these distinctions
explicit throughout.

We define the order parameter $K(x):=x^\top Gx$, the equilibrium shell $\{K=1\}$,
and the radial potential $V=\tfrac{1}{2}(K-1)^2$ with coordinate gradient
$\nabla_x V = 2(K-1)Gx$. Law~II takes the form
\[
  \dot x = (J_G - dI)\,\nabla_x V,\quad J_G := \alpha G^{-1}J_0,\quad d\ge 0,
\]
where damping is restricted to the isotropic class $D=dI$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Central new contribution.}
The companion paper~\cite{proto2d} noted that Class~B (tangential diffusion on
$\{K=1\}$) ``carries no heat'' and treated it as a gauge degree of freedom.
Section~\ref{sec:interface} of the present paper shows that the minimum symplectic
eigenvalue $\sigma_1 = \kappa\ell$ simultaneously determines the critical damping
scalar ($d_c = \alpha/\sigma_1$), the horizon area ($A = 4\pi\sigma_1^2$), and the
Bekenstein--Hawking entropy~\cite{Bekenstein,Hawking} ($S_\mathrm{BH} = \pi\sigma_1^2$), giving
Class~B the status of the \emph{symplectic geometric carrier of black-hole entropy}.
This is the unique formula-level feedback from the $(1{+}3)$-dimensional analysis
to the $2$-dimensional prototype.

$E[dW_t\,dW_t^\top]=I\,dt$ and $\sigma>0$.

\begin{theorem}[Block-product spectrum]
Let $G=\mathrm{diag}(g_0,g_1,g_2,g_3)$ and let $J$ be block-compatible with pairing
$\{i,j\}+\{k,l\}$. Then
\[
  \sigma(G^{-1}J) = \sigma(G_{ij}^{-1}J_2)\cup\sigma(G_{kl}^{-1}J_2),
\]
where the $2\times 2$ block characteristic polynomial is $\lambda^2+1/(g_i g_j)$.
Two pairings yield identical spectra iff their block-product multisets agree.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$G_{ij}^{-1}J_2=\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}0&1/g_i\\-1/g_j&0\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$,
$\det(\lambda I-G_{ij}^{-1}J_2)=\lambda^2+1/(g_i g_j)$.
Spectrum depends only on $g_i g_j$.\qed
\end{proof}

Consequences:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Rindler}: all three pairings equivalent ($g_1=g_2=g_3=-1$); $d_c=\alpha/(\kappa\ell)$.
\item \emph{Non-degenerate metrics}: pairings distinguished.
\item \emph{Schwarzschild} $G=\mathrm{diag}(-f,1/f,r^2,r^2\sin^2\theta)$:
      $J_0$ gives $d_c=\alpha$ (erases horizon info); $J_4$ gives
      $d_c=\alpha/(r\sqrt{f})\to\infty$ at the horizon.
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Observer-aligned cost $H_*$.} \textmd{[def]}
\[

\item \textbf{Radial OU variance.} \textmd{[verified]} $\Var(K)=\sigma^2/(2d_c)$.

\item \textbf{Tolman-type redshift structure.} \textmd{[verified]}

$T_\mathrm{loc}(\chi)\propto\cosh(2\chi)$.

Rindler $G=\mathrm{diag}((\kappa\ell)^2,-1,-1,-1)$:
$T_\mathrm{loc}(\chi)\propto(\kappa\ell)^2\cosh^2\chi+\sinh^2\chi$.

\end{enumerate}

\section{The symplectic--thermodynamics interface}
\label{sec:interface}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

This section provides the formula-level connection between the three structures
that remained separate in the $29$-item list above: the geometry of $H^3$, the
$\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ symmetry of $\{K=1\}$, and Bekenstein--Hawking thermodynamics.
It constitutes the principal new result of this paper relative to the
$2$-dimensional prototype~\cite{proto2d}.

\subsection{Symplectic area on $\{K=1\}$}

The minimum symplectic eigenvalue of the Rindler metric
$G=\mathrm{diag}((\kappa\ell)^2,-1,-1,-1)$ is $\sigma_1=\kappa\ell$
(item~20; proved via the block-product spectrum theorem, item~26).
Define the \emph{symplectic area} of $\{K=1\}$ by
\begin{equation}
  Q_{\mathrm{boost}} := \sigma_1^2 \cdot \mathrm{Area}(S^2)
  = (\kappa\ell)^2 \cdot 4\pi.
  \label{eq:Qboost_def}
\end{equation}
This is the symplectic analogue of the geometric horizon area: the
minimum symplectic eigenvalue $\sigma_1$ plays the role of the
horizon radius, and $4\pi$ is the solid angle of $S^2$.

\begin{remark}[Why not an integral of $\omega$?]\label{rem:omega_integral}
The standard symplectic $2$-form $\omega_0 = dx_0\wedge dx_1 + dx_2\wedge dx_3$
associated to $J_0$ restricts to $\{K=1\}$ as a $2$-form on~$H^3$, but its
$d\theta\wedge d\phi$ component at fixed~$\chi$ is proportional to
$\sin^2\!\theta\,\cos\phi$, which integrates to zero over~$S^2$:
\[
  \int_{S^2} \omega_0\big|_{\mathrm{fixed}\;\chi}
  \;\propto\; \int_0^{2\pi}\!\cos\phi\,d\phi = 0.
\]
This vanishing reflects the $J_0$ pairing structure $(0,1)+(2,3)$:
the form $\omega_0$ couples index~$2$ to index~$3$, producing a
$\phi$-dependent integrand on the $(1,2,3)$ sphere.
An integral representation of $Q_{\mathrm{boost}}$---if one exists---requires
either a different symplectic gauge or a Hodge-dual construction.
This is an open problem.
\end{remark}

\subsection{SO(1,3) Killing vectors and Class~B structure}

The three tangent directions of Class~B on $H^3$ correspond to $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$
generators as follows:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Tangent direction & SO(1,3) generator & Physical meaning\\
\midrule
$\partial_\chi$ & boost $K_1$ & horizon-normal boost\\
$\partial_\theta$ & rotation $L_{23}$ & sphere rotation\\
$\partial_\phi$ & rotation $L_{24}$ & sphere rotation\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Class~B diffusion on $\{K=1\}\cong H^3$ explores the degrees of freedom
associated with these generators. The entropy counted by $Q_\mathrm{boost}$
corresponds to the symplectic phase space accessible via boost diffusion.

\subsection{The central identity}

\begin{theorem}[Symplectic area = Bekenstein--Hawking entropy]
\label{thm:main}
For the Rindler metric $G=\mathrm{diag}((\kappa\ell)^2,-1,-1,-1)$,
\begin{equation}
  Q_{\mathrm{boost}}
  := \sigma_1^2 \cdot 4\pi
  = (\kappa\ell)^2 \cdot 4\pi
  = A_{\mathrm{horizon}}
  = 4\,S_{\mathrm{BH}}.
  \label{eq:main}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The block-product spectrum theorem (item~26) gives
$\sigma_1 = \kappa\ell$ as the minimum symplectic eigenvalue of~$G$.
The Rindler horizon area at unit normalisation is
$A_{\mathrm{horizon}} = 4\pi(\kappa\ell)^2 = 4\pi\sigma_1^2$.
Bekenstein--Hawking entropy is $S_{\mathrm{BH}} = A_{\mathrm{horizon}}/4
= \pi\sigma_1^2$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:main} follows.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Status of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
The identity $Q_{\mathrm{boost}} = 4S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ is algebraic:
it equates the symplectic area $\sigma_1^2\cdot 4\pi$ with the
geometric horizon area $A_{\mathrm{horizon}}$.  Both sides are
determined by the single quantity~$\sigma_1 = \kappa\ell$.
The theorem does not require an integral representation of
$Q_{\mathrm{boost}}$ as a symplectic flux; finding such a
representation is an open problem
(Remark~\ref{rem:omega_integral}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[One symplectic invariant controls three quantities]
\label{rem:sigma1}
The minimum symplectic eigenvalue $\sigma_1$ of the Hessian metric~$G$
simultaneously determines:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] the critical damping scalar: $d_c = \alpha/\sigma_1$ (item~20),
\item[(ii)] the horizon area: $A = 4\pi\sigma_1^2$,
\item[(iii)] the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy: $S_{\mathrm{BH}} = \pi\sigma_1^2$.
\end{enumerate}
All three are controlled by a single symplectic invariant.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Geometric reinterpretation of Class~B]
The companion paper~\cite{proto2d} described Class~B as ``a gauge degree of freedom''
carrying no heat. Theorem~\ref{thm:main} identifies the symplectic area
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$, giving Class~B tangential diffusion on $H^3$
the status of the \emph{symplectic geometric carrier of black-hole entropy}.
In two dimensions $\{K=1\}$ is a one-dimensional hyperbola and carries no area;
the entropy content of Class~B emerges only in $(1{+}3)$ dimensions where
$\{K=1\}\cong H^3$ is three-dimensional.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Geometric interpretation of Var$(K)$}

Under the noise-temperature matching of item~15:
\begin{equation}
  \Var(K) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2d_c} = T_\mathrm{tol}
           = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi\ell},
  \label{eq:varK_geometric}
\end{equation}
where the last equality uses the $\kappa$-cancellation
$d_c\cdot T_H = \alpha\kappa/(2\pi\kappa\ell) = \alpha/(2\pi\ell)$.
Combining with $Q_\mathrm{boost} = \sigma_1^2\cdot 4\pi = (\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi$:
\begin{equation}
  \Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost}
  = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi\ell}\cdot(\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi
  = \frac{2\alpha\,\sigma_1^2}{\ell}.
  \label{eq:varK_Qboost}
\end{equation}
Using the interface identity $d_c\cdot\sigma_1 = \alpha$ (item~20),
this can be rewritten as
\begin{equation}
  \Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost}
  = 2\,d_c\,\sigma_1^3 / \ell.
  \label{eq:varK_Qboost_alt}
\end{equation}

\begin{remark}[Dimensionless form and special cases]
\label{rem:dimless}
In units where $\sigma_1 = \kappa\ell = 1$ (the stretched-horizon convention
$\ell = 1/\kappa$), equation~\eqref{eq:varK_Qboost} reduces to
$\Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost} = 2\alpha\ell$.
More generally, the product scales as $\sigma_1^2/\ell$: it
increases with horizon area and decreases with proper distance.
Equations~\eqref{eq:varK_Qboost}--\eqref{eq:varK_Qboost_alt} are
numerically verified for $\kappa\ell\in\{0.5,1,2,3\}$ at $\ell=\sigma_1/\kappa$.
\end{remark}

The chain is:
\emph{statistical fluctuation ($\Var(K)$)
$\;\leftrightarrow\;$
symplectic eigenvalue ($\sigma_1$)
$\;\leftrightarrow\;$
horizon area ($Q_\mathrm{boost}$)
$\;\leftrightarrow\;$
Bekenstein--Hawking entropy ($S_\mathrm{BH}$)}.
All four are controlled by~$\sigma_1$.

\begin{remark}[Correction to the 2-dimensional prototype]
\label{rem:correction}
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} implies that Class~B
tangential diffusion carries Bekenstein--Hawking entropy via
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$, giving new content to the Class~B sector
that was absent in the 2D setting. In two dimensions $\{K=1\}$ is a
one-dimensional hyperbola with no area to integrate; the description
``carries no heat'' in~\cite{proto2d} is correct in 2D.
The $(1{+}3)$-dimensional promotion $\{K=1\}\cong H^3$ introduces
$\mathrm{Area}(S^2) = 4\pi$, and the symplectic area
$Q_\mathrm{boost} = \sigma_1^2\cdot 4\pi$ acquires thermodynamic content.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Scope and open problems}

\begin{remark}[Non-degenerate metrics]
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} is proved for Rindler $G$, where spatial components are
degenerate ($g_1=g_2=g_3=-1$) and all three symplectic pairings give the same $d_c$.
For non-degenerate metrics (Schwarzschild, Kerr), the block-product spectrum theorem
shows that different pairings give distinct $d_c$ (item~26(iii)). Whether
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$ holds for Schwarzschild under the appropriate
pairing choice requires computing $\sigma_1$ at the horizon $r=2M$;
this is left for future work.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Integral representation]
The symplectic area $Q_\mathrm{boost} = \sigma_1^2\cdot 4\pi$ is defined
algebraically via the minimum symplectic eigenvalue (Definition~\eqref{eq:Qboost_def}).
Whether it admits an integral representation as a symplectic flux---for instance
via a modified symplectic form, a Hodge-dual construction, or a pairing other
than $J_0$---is an open problem (Remark~\ref{rem:omega_integral}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[SO(3) rotational charges]
The two rotational Killing vectors $\partial_\theta,\partial_\phi$ generate Noether
charges whose thermodynamic interpretation (angular momentum, Kerr entropy) is not
addressed here and constitutes an open problem.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Global measure]
The full $H^3$ volume $\int_0^\infty\sinh^2\chi\,d\chi\cdot 4\pi$ diverges.
This is the geometric explanation for the absence of a globally normalisable
stationary density in the full $4$D SDE (item~17): the $\chi$-direction random walk
has infinite volume to explore. The finite quantity is $Q_\mathrm{boost}$, obtained
