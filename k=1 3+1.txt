\begin{abstract}
We present a $(1{+}3)$-dimensional formulation of K\,=\,1 Chronogeometrodynamics
whose central feature is a symplectic gauge collapse from continuous $6$-dimensional
freedom to a discrete three-element pairing class, under the local-inertial-frame
constraint that the cost weight $H_*$ is diagonal. The framework defines an information
time $dt_\mathrm{info} := d\Phi/H$, a Hessian metric $G = \nabla^2(dt_\mathrm{info}^2)|_{K=1}$,
and studies the dynamics of the order parameter $K(x) := x^\top G x$ near the
equilibrium shell $\{K=1\}$.

Three declared inputs are separated from internal deductions throughout:
(i)~Lorentzian signature $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$, established in a companion realizability
paper; (ii)~a canonical symplectic gauge $J_0$, selected by the Route~1 Proposition,
which reduces the $6$-dimensional gauge freedom to a discrete $3$-element set under
the diagonal-$H_*$ constraint; (iii)~an isotropic It\^o SDE for fluctuation analysis.

Within these inputs the internal chain is logically closed. The unique symplectic
generator $J_G = \alpha G^{-1}J_0$ yields a critical damping scalar
$d_c = \alpha/(\kappa\ell)$ for Rindler metrics. In the local radial OU sector
with isotropic noise, $\Var(K)=\sigma^2/(2d_c)$ universally across all Lorentzian $G$.
A Clausius relation $\delta Q = T_\mathrm{eff}\,\delta S$ holds only in the reduced
Class~A radial sector.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{New result (Section~\ref{sec:interface}).}
The Class~B tangential diffusion on $\{K=1\} \cong H^3$ carries a conserved
SO$(1,3)$ Noether charge
\[
  Q_\mathrm{boost} := \int_{S^2} \iota_{\partial_\chi}\,\omega\big|_{\{K=1\}}
  = (\kappa\ell)^2 \cdot 4\pi = A_\mathrm{horizon} = 4\,S_\mathrm{BH}.
\]
This identifies Class~B not as a gauge degree of freedom but as the symplectic
geometric carrier of Bekenstein--Hawking entropy. Combined with
$\Var(K) = \sigma^2/(2d_c) = T_\mathrm{tol}$, the variance formula acquires a
geometric interpretation: it equals the Tolman temperature, which is itself
expressed via the symplectic area $Q_\mathrm{boost}/(4\pi)$ on~$H^3$.
The J$_0$/J$_4$ equivalence in the Rindler/$2{+}2$ sector is proved algebraically
via the block-product spectrum theorem.
Several identities are numerically verified on five standard Lorentzian metrics.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The $2$-dimensional prototype of K\,=\,1 Chronogeometrodynamics~\cite{proto2d}
established a tight logical chain: from the information-time definition and the
Hessian metric $G$, one derives (i)~a unique symplectic generator $J_G$ (in 2D the
skew space is one-dimensional, so no gauge choice is needed), (ii)~a critical damping
scalar $d_c$ that is positive if and only if $\Sig(G)=(1,1)$, and (iii)~a Clausius
relation in the radial fluctuation sector. In 2D the three conditions $d_c>0$,
$\det G<0$, and $\Sig=(1,1)$ are mutually equivalent; uniqueness of $J_G$ requires
no additional input.

Moving to $(1{+}3)$ dimensions changes the logical structure in two important ways.
First, the skew-symmetric matrix space is six-dimensional, so $G$ alone does not
determine $J_0$ uniquely; a gauge choice is required. Second, the spectral condition
$d_c>0$ is no longer equivalent to $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$: Lorentzian signature implies
$d_c>0$, but $d_c>0$ does not imply $\Sig(G)=(1,3)$, since for example
$\Sig=(3,1)$ also yields $d_c>0$. The present paper makes these distinctions
explicit throughout.

We define the order parameter $K(x):=x^\top Gx$, the equilibrium shell $\{K=1\}$,
and the radial potential $V=\tfrac{1}{2}(K-1)^2$ with coordinate gradient
$\nabla_x V = 2(K-1)Gx$. Law~II takes the form
\[
  \dot x = (J_G - dI)\,\nabla_x V,\quad J_G := \alpha G^{-1}J_0,\quad d\ge 0,
\]
where damping is restricted to the isotropic class $D=dI$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Central new contribution.}
The companion paper~\cite{proto2d} noted that Class~B (tangential diffusion on
$\{K=1\}$) ``carries no heat'' and treated it as a gauge degree of freedom.
Section~\ref{sec:interface} of the present paper overturns this interpretation:
the $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ Noether charge of the boost Killing vector restricted to
$\{K=1\}\cong H^3$ equals the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy, giving
Class~B the status of the \emph{symplectic geometric carrier of black-hole entropy}.
This is the unique formula-level feedback from the $(1{+}3)$-dimensional analysis
to the $2$-dimensional prototype.

$E[dW_t\,dW_t^\top]=I\,dt$ and $\sigma>0$.

\begin{theorem}[Block-product spectrum]
Let $G=\mathrm{diag}(g_0,g_1,g_2,g_3)$ and let $J$ be block-compatible with pairing
$\{i,j\}+\{k,l\}$. Then
\[
  \sigma(G^{-1}J) = \sigma(G_{ij}^{-1}J_2)\cup\sigma(G_{kl}^{-1}J_2),
\]
where the $2\times 2$ block characteristic polynomial is $\lambda^2+1/(g_i g_j)$.
Two pairings yield identical spectra iff their block-product multisets agree.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$G_{ij}^{-1}J_2=\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}0&1/g_i\\-1/g_j&0\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$,
$\det(\lambda I-G_{ij}^{-1}J_2)=\lambda^2+1/(g_i g_j)$.
Spectrum depends only on $g_i g_j$.\qed
\end{proof}

Consequences:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Rindler}: all three pairings equivalent ($g_1=g_2=g_3=1$); $d_c=1/(\kappa\xi)$.
\item \emph{Non-degenerate metrics}: pairings distinguished.
\item \emph{Schwarzschild} $G=\mathrm{diag}(-f,1/f,r^2,r^2\sin^2\theta)$:
      $J_0$ gives $d_c=1$ (erases horizon info); $J_4$ gives
      $d_c=1/(r\sqrt{f})\to\infty$ at the horizon.
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Observer-aligned cost $H_*$.} \textmd{[def]}
\[

\]

\item \textbf{Radial OU variance.} \textmd{[verified]} $\Var(K)=\sigma^2/(2d_c)$.

\item \textbf{Tolman-type redshift structure.} \textmd{[verified]}

Minkowski $G=\mathrm{diag}(1,-1,-1,-1)$:
$T_\mathrm{loc}(\chi)\propto\cosh(2\chi)$.

Rindler $G=\mathrm{diag}((\kappa\ell)^2,-1,-1,-1)$:
$T_\mathrm{loc}(\chi)\propto(\kappa\ell)^2\cosh^2\chi+\sinh^2\chi$.

\end{enumerate}

\section{The symplectic--thermodynamics interface}
\label{sec:interface}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

This section provides the formula-level connection between the three structures
that remained separate in the $29$-item list above: the geometry of $H^3$, the
$\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ symmetry of $\{K=1\}$, and Bekenstein--Hawking thermodynamics.
It constitutes the principal new result of this paper relative to the
$2$-dimensional prototype~\cite{proto2d}.

\subsection{Symplectic 2-form on $H^3$}

The symplectic form associated to $J_G=\alpha G^{-1}J_0$ is
\[
  \omega = \tfrac{1}{2}\,dx^\top (GJ_0 - J_0^\top G)\,dx.
\]
Restricted to $\{K=1\}$ with the Rindler parametrisation of item~19,
the induced $2$-form on each constant-$\chi$ sphere $S^2$ is
\begin{equation}
  \omega_{S^2} = \sigma_1^2\,\sin\theta\,d\theta\wedge d\phi
              = (\kappa\ell)^2\,\sin\theta\,d\theta\wedge d\phi,
  \label{eq:omegaS2}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_1=\kappa\ell$ is the minimum symplectic eigenvalue of $G$ (item~20).

\subsection{SO(1,3) Killing vectors and Noether charges}

The three tangent directions of Class~B on $H^3$ correspond to $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$
generators as follows:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Tangent direction & SO(1,3) generator & Physical meaning\\
\midrule
$\partial_\chi$ & boost $K_1$ & horizon-normal boost\\
$\partial_\theta$ & rotation $L_{23}$ & sphere rotation\\
$\partial_\phi$ & rotation $L_{24}$ & sphere rotation\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The Noether charge of the boost Killing vector $\xi=\partial_\chi$ is
\begin{equation}
  Q_\mathrm{boost} := \int_{S^2} \iota_{\partial_\chi}\,\omega\big|_{\{K=1\}}.
  \label{eq:Qboost_def}
\end{equation}

\subsection{The central identity}

\begin{theorem}[Symplectic area = Bekenstein--Hawking entropy]
\label{thm:main}
For the Rindler metric $G=\mathrm{diag}((\kappa\ell)^2,-1,-1,-1)$ with canonical
gauge $J_0$,
\begin{equation}
  Q_\mathrm{boost}
  = \int_{S^2} \iota_{\partial_\chi}\,\omega\big|_{\{K=1\}}
  = (\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi
  = A_\mathrm{horizon}
  = 4\,S_\mathrm{BH}.
  \label{eq:main}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \eqref{eq:omegaS2},
\[
  \int_{S^2}\omega_{S^2}
  = (\kappa\ell)^2\int_0^\pi\sin\theta\,d\theta\int_0^{2\pi}d\phi
  = (\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi.
\]
The interior product $\iota_{\partial_\chi}\,\omega$ evaluated on the $(\theta,\phi)$
plane at fixed $\chi$ gives exactly $\omega_{S^2}$, since $\partial_\chi$ is the
boost direction normal to~$S^2$ and $\omega$ pairs the boost with the sphere area
element via the block structure of~$J_0$.

For the Rindler metric at unit normalisation, the horizon area is
$A_\mathrm{horizon}=4\pi(\kappa\ell)^2$ and Bekenstein--Hawking entropy is
$S_\mathrm{BH}=A_\mathrm{horizon}/4=\pi(\kappa\ell)^2$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:main} follows.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Geometric reinterpretation of Class~B]
The companion paper~\cite{proto2d} described Class~B as ``a gauge degree of freedom''
carrying no heat. Theorem~\ref{thm:main} overturns this at the formula level:
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$ identifies the SO$(1,3)$ Noether charge of Class~B
with four times the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy. Class~B tangential diffusion on $H^3$
is therefore the \emph{symplectic geometric carrier of black-hole entropy}, not a
redundant degree of freedom to be discarded.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Geometric interpretation of Var$(K)$}

The variance formula (item~13) acquires a new geometric meaning via
Theorem~\ref{thm:main}. Under the noise-temperature matching of item~15:
\begin{equation}
  \Var(K) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2d_c} = T_\mathrm{tol}
           = d_c\cdot T_H = \frac{1}{2\pi\ell}.
  \label{eq:varK_geometric}
\end{equation}
From item~20, $d_c=\alpha/\sigma_1=\alpha/(\kappa\ell)$, so
\[
  T_\mathrm{tol} = \frac{\alpha}{\kappa\ell}\cdot\frac{\kappa}{2\pi}
                 = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi\ell}.
\]
Combining with \eqref{eq:main} and $Q_\mathrm{boost}=(\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi$:
\begin{equation}
  \Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost}
  = T_\mathrm{tol}\cdot(\kappa\ell)^2\cdot 4\pi
  = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi\ell}\cdot 4\pi\ell^2
  = 2\alpha\ell.
  \label{eq:varK_Qboost}
\end{equation}
Equivalently, $\Var(K) = \alpha/(2\pi\ell) = 2\alpha\ell/Q_\mathrm{boost}$.
The product $\Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost}=2\alpha\ell$ is numerically verified
for $\kappa\ell\in\{0.5,1,2,3\}$ and encodes the geometric closure:
\emph{statistical fluctuation $\times$ symplectic horizon area $= 2\alpha\times$proper distance}.
This closes the chain: \emph{statistical fluctuation $\leftrightarrow$ symplectic
geometry $\leftrightarrow$ thermodynamic entropy}.

\begin{remark}[Correction to the 2-dimensional prototype]
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} and equation~\eqref{eq:varK_Qboost} imply that Class~B
tangential diffusion carries Bekenstein--Hawking entropy via
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$, overturning the earlier description of Class~B
as ``carrying no heat'' in~\cite{proto2d}. The full amended statement---including
equations $\delta Q_\mathrm{ClassB}=T_\mathrm{tol}\,\delta S_\mathrm{BH}$ and
the geometric closure $\Var(K)\cdot Q_\mathrm{boost}=2\alpha\ell$
in the $2$-dimensional limit---is incorporated in the updated 2D
paper~\cite{proto2d} as Remarks~18--19 and equation~(geo\_closure) therein.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Scope and open problems}

\begin{remark}[Non-degenerate metrics]
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} is proved for Rindler $G$, where spatial components are
degenerate ($g_1=g_2=g_3=1$) and all three symplectic pairings give the same $d_c$.
For non-degenerate metrics (Schwarzschild, Kerr), the block-product spectrum theorem
shows that different pairings give distinct $d_c$ (item~26(iii)). Whether
$Q_\mathrm{boost}=4S_\mathrm{BH}$ holds for Schwarzschild under the $J_0$ gauge
requires computing $\int_{S^2}\iota_{\partial_r}\omega$ on the horizon $r=2M$;
this is left for~\cite{pairing}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[SO(3) rotational charges]
The two rotational Killing vectors $\partial_\theta,\partial_\phi$ generate Noether
charges
\[
  Q_L = \int_{S^1}\iota_{\partial_\theta}\,\omega\big|_{S^2}.
\]
Their thermodynamic interpretation (angular momentum, Kerr entropy) is not addressed
here and constitutes an open problem.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Global measure]
The full $H^3$ volume $\int_0^\infty\sinh^2\chi\,d\chi\cdot 4\pi$ diverges.
This is the geometric explanation for the absence of a globally normalisable
stationary density in the full $4$D SDE (item~17): the $\chi$-direction random walk
has infinite volume to explore. The finite quantity is $Q_\mathrm{boost}$, obtained
by fixing $\chi$ and integrating over $S^2$ only.
\end{remark}
