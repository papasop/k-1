Abstract

Background. Time is usually presupposed. Here it is modeled as information cost: dt_info = dΦ / H. The associated leading quadratic form G is constrained by the realizability axioms R, E, T and non-degeneracy.

Point-level result. In 2D, those conditions force Sig(G) = (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. Under isotropic linearization, Lorentzian signature, real spectrum of J_G, and a positive stability boundary d_c > 0 are equivalent. The unit-cost locus is K(x)=xᵀGx=1.

Dynamics. Within the symplectic–dissipative class GA = αJ − GD, the unique law is ẋ = (J_G − D)∇V; at critical damping and a normalized eigenvector base point, the local restoring rate is κ_K = 4 d_c.

Gravity. Two routes connect to Einstein’s equations: Route A via local stochastic thermodynamics and Jacobson’s argument, requiring external inputs T_eff = T_tol and S ∝ A; Route B gives, in static spherical symmetry, an algebraic equivalence between the field-level K=1 analogue and R_{μν}=0.

§1 · Law I

From information time to Lorentzian type

The first claim is algebraic and local: the cost structure fixes the type of the leading quadratic form. The figure below lets you drag the determinant across its sign change and watch the geometric, spectral, and dynamical reading move together.

Framework

Define information time as dt_info := dΦ/H, where Φ parametrizes structural variation and H > 0 is a local scalar cost per unit variation. The signed principal quadratic form associated with dt_info² is modeled as G ≡ Q, the leading form of the realizability paper.

Axioms R, E, T, together with non-degeneracy, exclude both definite signatures in two dimensions. The only remaining inertia is therefore (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. This does not determine the entries of G; it determines the Lorentzian algebraic type.

Once G is fixed, it defines K(x) = xᵀGx. The additional condition K = 1 is not inherited from the realizability axioms; it is the paper’s own self-consistency condition and becomes dynamically meaningful only after Laws II and III are added.

§2 · Laws II–III

Unique dynamics and the restoring law

Law II fixes the admissible symplectic–dissipative class. Law III then gives the local restoring structure of K=1. The audit below lets you move among stable, marginal, and unstable regimes while watching the local theorem conditions switch on and off.

Statement

With J_G = αG⁻¹J, the canonical law inside the admissible class GA = αJ − GD is ẋ = (J_G − D)∇V. Choosing the penalty V = ½(K−1)² makes deviations from the unit-cost locus dynamically costly.

At critical damping D=d_c I, and at a base point on K=1 that is also a G-eigenvector, the drift linearizes to Ḱ = −κ_K(K−1)+o(K−1). If the base point is normalized by x_*ᵀG²x_*=1, the coefficient collapses to κ_K = 4d_c.

§3 · Two routes

One derivation, one equivalence theorem

The page separates the two roads clearly. Route A is thermodynamic and needs external input. Route B is purely algebraic but presently restricted to static spherical symmetry. The central question is not whether they look similar, but whether they meet cleanly in the vacuum sector.

Difference

Route A uses local stochastic dynamics near K=1, the Clausius relation, the external identification T_eff=T_tol, and then Jacobson’s argument. Its output is the Einstein equation with matter, but that output still depends on external thermodynamic inputs.

Route B is different in nature. It does not derive Einstein’s equations from the realizability axioms. Instead, in the static spherically symmetric vacuum sector, it proves that the field-level K=1 analogue is algebraically equivalent to the vacuum Einstein equations. It is therefore an equivalence theorem, not an alternative route to all of GR.

The interactive consistency check below deliberately uses the one setting where both roads meet without conceptual slippage: Schwarzschild vacuum.

§4 · Proof map

Theorem 5.2 as an executable equivalence

Instead of reprinting the proof passively, the page turns the algebraic steps into a constrained map. Change the profile, move along the premise boundary, or switch off spherical symmetry, and the theorem either remains exact or becomes explicitly inapplicable.

Core identities

For a static spherical metric ds² = −fdt² + f⁻¹dr² + r²dΩ², the two field-level self-consistency functionals are

K₁ = f + 2rf′ + ½r²f″andK₂ = f + rf′.

These quantities are not decorative. They lock directly to the Ricci components: R_{θθ}=1−K₂, R = 2(1−K₁)/r², and R_{tt}=f(K₁−K₂)/r². The theorem lives entirely in the exact conjunction of these identities.

§6 · Audits

Where the page refuses to overclaim

The point of an interactive paper is not to make every slider tell a success story. It is to make the points of failure explicit, in exactly the places where the written paper itself imposes scope conditions, external inputs, or unresolved open problems.

§7 · Λ extension

A controlled Einstein-space shift

Vacuum is not the end of the story, but the next step must be clean. The page therefore treats the cosmological constant as a separate extension of the spherical theorem, with its own shifted targets for K₁ and K₂.

Formula

For Schwarzschild–de Sitter one has f(r)=1−2M/r−Λr²/3. The two legs shift differently:

K₁ = 1 − 2Λr²andK₂ = 1 − Λr².

The extension therefore keeps the asymmetry visible. It is not enough to say “the theorem still works with Λ”; one must specify exactly how each leg moves, and then check that those shifts are equivalent to R_{μν} = Λ g_{μν}.

§8 · RN near-horizon / extremal

Why Reissner–Nordström is a one-leg example, not a vacuum branch

A common temptation is to treat RN as though it were merely a second vacuum branch of the K=1 framework. The page forbids that shortcut: RN lives here as a controlled example of what it looks like when one leg survives and the other does not.

Interpretation

For the RN-type profile f(r)=1−2M/r+Q²/r², the first leg remains exactly at K₁=1. But the second leg becomes K₂ = 1 − Q²/r², so the angular Ricci channel remains sourced. The page therefore treats RN as a useful stress test for asymmetry, not as a counterexample to the vacuum theorem.

Near extremality the outer horizon moves toward r_+=M, but this only sharpens the lesson: the spherical vacuum endpoint is still unavailable until the second leg closes as well.

§9 · Leg asymmetry

Rigid K₂, flexible K₁

One of the paper’s key structural clarifications is that the two legs are not interchangeable. This section turns that asymmetry into something you can drag, freeze, and inspect directly.

Exact statement

If K₂ ≡ 1 on an open interval, then the ODE integrates to f=1+C/r, and hence K₁ ≡ 1 automatically. But if K₁ ≡ 1, the general solution is the two-parameter family f=1+A/r+B/r², for which K₂ = 1 − B/r².

So the failure is one-sided: K₂=1 ⇒ K₁=1 exactly, but K₁=1 alone still leaves a non-vacuum branch open whenever B ≠ 0.

§10 · Birkhoff ladder

From one leg to Schwarzschild via an ODE collapse

This three-stage ladder turns the static spherical vacuum reduction into a visible compression of degrees of freedom. First one leg leaves a two-parameter family; then the second leg kills the extra constant; finally the remaining parameter is read as mass.

Why keep this?

This is not the full Birkhoff theorem. The panel begins only after staticity and spherical symmetry are already imposed. Its purpose is narrower and cleaner: to show how the field-level self-consistency equations collapse the static family from two constants to one, and why Schwarzschild is thereby singled out inside that sector.

Because this panel is easy to overstate, it now ends with a “what it validates / what it does not validate” box. The mass readout is internal to the reduced family; the standard Hawking relation remains a standard external physical relation, not a new theorem of the present framework.

§11 · Closing

An interactive paper should make scope visible

The strongest use of interactivity is not to make the argument look more magical. It is to make every premise, reduction, and scope condition harder to forget.

Takeaway

The page now enforces the same discipline as the written paper. Route A stays thermodynamic and explicitly input-dependent. Route B stays an exact spherical vacuum equivalence theorem, plus separately labeled extensions. The one-leg asymmetry is explicit. RN is shown as a non-vacuum example, not a vacuum loophole. The Λ shift is treated as its own corollary. The Birkhoff ladder is guarded against over-reading.

If the page is doing its job, dragging a slider cannot silently overstate a theorem. It can only move within the paper’s own domains, identities, and declared limitations.
