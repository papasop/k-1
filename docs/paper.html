Abstract

Background. Time is usually presupposed. Here it is modeled as information cost: dt_info = dΦ / H. The associated leading quadratic form G is constrained by the realizability axioms R, E, T and non-degeneracy.

Point-level result. In 2D, those conditions force Sig(G) = (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. Under isotropic linearization, Lorentzian signature, real spectrum of J_G, and a positive stability boundary d_c > 0 are equivalent. The unit-cost locus is K(x)=xᵀGx=1.

Dynamics. Within the symplectic–dissipative class GA = αJ − GD, the unique law is ẋ = (J_G − D)∇V; at critical damping and a normalized eigenvector base point, the local restoring rate is κ_K = 4 d_c.

Gravity. Two routes connect to Einstein’s equations: Route A via local stochastic thermodynamics and Jacobson’s argument, requiring external inputs T_eff = T_tol and S ∝ A; Route B gives, in static spherical symmetry, an algebraic equivalence between the field-level K=1 analogue and R_{μν}=0.

Framework

Define information time as dt_info := dΦ/H, where Φ parametrizes structural variation and H > 0 is a local scalar cost per unit variation. The signed principal quadratic form associated with dt_info² is modeled as G ≡ Q, the leading form of the realizability paper.

Axioms R, E, T, together with non-degeneracy, exclude both definite signatures in two dimensions. The only remaining inertia is therefore (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. This does not determine the entries of G; it determines the Lorentzian algebraic type.

Once G is fixed, it defines K(x) = xᵀGx. The additional condition K = 1 is not inherited from the realizability axioms; it is the paper’s own self-consistency condition and becomes dynamically meaningful only after Laws II and III are added.
