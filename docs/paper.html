Abstract

Background. Time is usually presupposed. Here it is modeled as information cost: dt_info = dΦ / H. The associated leading quadratic form G is constrained by the realizability axioms R, E, T and non-degeneracy.

Point-level result. In 2D, those conditions force Sig(G) = (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. Under isotropic linearization, Lorentzian signature, real spectrum of J_G, and a positive stability boundary d_c > 0 are equivalent. The unit-cost locus is K(x)=xᵀGx=1.

Dynamics. Within the symplectic–dissipative class GA = αJ − GD, the unique law is ẋ = (J_G − D)∇V; at critical damping and a normalized eigenvector base point, the local restoring rate is κ_K = 4 d_c.

Gravity. Two routes connect to Einstein’s equations: Route A via local stochastic thermodynamics and Jacobson’s argument, requiring external inputs T_eff = T_tol and S ∝ A; Route B gives, in static spherical symmetry, an algebraic equivalence between the field-level K=1 analogue and R_{μν}=0.

§1 · Law I

From information time to Lorentzian type

The first claim is algebraic and local: the cost structure fixes the type of the leading quadratic form. The figure below lets you drag the determinant across its sign change and watch the geometric, spectral, and dynamical reading move together.

Framework

Define information time as dt_info := dΦ/H, where Φ parametrizes structural variation and H > 0 is a local scalar cost per unit variation. The signed principal quadratic form associated with dt_info² is modeled as G ≡ Q, the leading form of the realizability paper.

Axioms R, E, T, together with non-degeneracy, exclude both definite signatures in two dimensions. The only remaining inertia is therefore (1,1), equivalently det G < 0. This does not determine the entries of G; it determines the Lorentzian algebraic type.

Once G is fixed, it defines K(x) = xᵀGx. The additional condition K = 1 is not inherited from the realizability axioms; it is the paper’s own self-consistency condition and becomes dynamically meaningful only after Laws II and III are added.

§2 · Laws II–III

Dynamics and the local restoring law

The theorem-level point is subtle: K=1 is not just a level set. Under the unique symplectic–dissipative law and at critical damping, deviations from K=1 are restored locally with rate κK=4dc.

Interpretation

Law II imposes the structural split GA = αJ − GD with D≥0; in this class the unique generator is A = J_G − D. Law III specializes to the critical isotropic choice D = d_c I and studies the resulting nonlinear drift for V = ½(K−1)².

§3 · Two Routes

One thermodynamic bridge, one algebraic equivalence

The paper makes a clean distinction. Route A recovers Einstein’s equations using local Clausius plus Jacobson, and therefore needs external physical input. Route B is independent of thermodynamics but presently confined to static spherical symmetry.

§11 · Closing

What this page does and does not claim

The page mirrors the paper’s scope. It turns the major logical dependencies into visible objects and test benches, but it does not promote the paper into a larger claim than the text itself supports.

Takeaway

The local content of the paper is sharp: realizability gives Lorentzian type, the symplectic–dissipative law makes K=1 dynamically meaningful, Route A provides a thermodynamic bridge contingent on standard physical input, and Route B yields an algebraic equivalence to the spherical vacuum equations.

The nonlocal and fully general program remains open. General metrics, hard lower bounds on σ₁, and global attractivity of K=1 are not solved here. The point of the interactive format is to prevent those open edges from being visually flattened into conclusions.
